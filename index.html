Central Route

Blast the flatlands. Savor the West.

Three big driving days across the eastern interstates, then the Colorado Rockies and Utah's red rock back-to-back. The big-views, big-nature option.

~2,800Miles
7Days
11States
4Nat'l Parks

👍 Pros

  • Densest run of big-view nature in the country — Rockies, Arches, Canyonlands, Zion, almost back-to-back.
  • Cool mountain relief mid-trip; a welcome break from summer heat (Glenwood sits at 5,700 ft).
  • The most direct path west from New York — you only drop south at the very end.
  • Relaxed western pacing with shorter daily drives once you hit Colorado.

👎 Cons

  • Skips the Grand Canyon entirely.
  • The eastern half (Ohio, Iowa, Nebraska) is a long, flat grind with modest stops.
  • Good food en route, but no destination food cities.
  • Day 3 (Des Moines → Denver) is a ~10-hour haul.

Central Route · Know Before You Go

What makes or breaks it

🎆 July 4th weekend

Book Moab (Jul 3) & Springdale (Jul 4) ASAP — they sell out months ahead and prices surge.

🚗 Beat the Jul 5 I-15 jam

Clear Las Vegas before ~1 PM to dodge hours of standstill near Cajon Pass.

🎟️ America the Beautiful pass

$80/yr covers Colorado Nat'l Monument, Arches, Canyonlands & Zion — pays for itself.

📅 Reservations & shuttles

Arches timed-entry (Jul 3) + the Zion shuttle are the two logistics that trip people up.

💧 Respect the desert

Days 5–7 hit 100–110°F. Tons of water, hike at sunrise, tank above half in the Mojave.

Southern Route

Music, BBQ & the Southwest.

Down through Nashville and Memphis, across to Santa Fe and Sedona. The food-and-culture option — and a longer haul. Cleaned up to realistic distances, it's a true 7 days.

~3,060Miles
7Days
10States
2Nat'l Parks

👍 Pros

  • The best food-and-culture corridor anywhere — Nashville, Memphis, Santa Fe.
  • Huge variety of interesting stops: New River Gorge, Bathhouse Row, Cadillac Ranch, Route 66, Sedona.
  • Two Southwest icons in one trip — Santa Fe's adobe culture and Sedona's red rocks.
  • The middle days (Nashville, Memphis, Santa Fe) are destinations in their own right.

👎 Cons

  • Longest route (~3,060 mi) with two unavoidable ~9-hour days (Days 1 & 4).
  • Peak summer heat with little altitude relief on the driving corridors; the final desert stretch bakes.
  • Long flat stretches (Arkansas, Oklahoma, the Texas panhandle) between highlights.
  • Skips the Grand Canyon, and ends in San Diego — ~1 hr south of Laguna Niguel.

Southern Route · Know Before You Go

What makes or breaks it

📏 Mind the real distances

The route is ~3,060 mi — double-check each leg in Maps. Days 1 & 4 are the long ~9-hour hauls.

🎆 July 4th lodging

Sedona & Santa Fe over the holiday are busy and pricey — book those nights first.

💧 Desert + heat

The OK/TX corridor and the final AZ→CA stretch bake in July. Hydrate, fuel often, start early.

🍽️ Reserve the food cities

Café Pasqual's (Santa Fe) and Elote (Sedona) draw lines — reserve or go early.

🧭 Endpoint

This lands in San Diego; add ~60 mi / 1 hr north up the coast to reach Laguna Niguel.
